I am an undergraduate student in Industrial & Systems Engineering at the University of Cincinnati, with minors in Mathematics and Statistics. My academic interests are in operations research, optimization under uncertainty, stochastic optimization, bilevel optimization, and derivative-free optimization.

I am interested in both the theory behind optimization algorithms and how these methods can be applied to practical problems in healthcare operations, stochastic supply chains, and machine learning. I have worked on research projects involving bilevel optimization for neural architecture search and stochastic models for flexible stochastic supply chains.

## Research interests

- Operations research
- Optimization under uncertainty
- Stochastic optimization
- Bilevel optimization
- Derivative-free optimization
- Healthcare operations, stochastic supply chains, and machine learning applications

## Current work

I am currently working on research at the intersection of stochastic optimization, bilevel optimization, and decision-making under uncertainty. My recent projects include derivative-free bilevel search methods for neural architecture search and simulation-based models of flexibility in stochastic supply chains.
