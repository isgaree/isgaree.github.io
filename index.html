About me

I am an undergraduate student in Industrial & Systems Engineering at the University of Cincinnati, with minors in Mathematics and Statistics. My research interests center on optimization under uncertainty, including bilevel, stochastic, and derivative-free optimization methods.

I am interested in both the theory behind optimization algorithms and how these methods can be applied to practical problems in healthcare operations, supply chains, and machine learning. I have worked on research projects involving bilevel optimization for neural architecture search and stochastic models for flexible supply chains.

Projects

Stochastic Methods for Bilevel Optimization

Undergraduate research · University of Cincinnati

I work on bilevel optimization methods with a focus on derivative-free and stochastic approaches. My work has involved implementing and comparing bilevel algorithms for neural architecture search, including DARTS, StocBiO, and derivative-free bilevel search methods.

Bilevel optimizationNeural architecture searchPyTorch

3D Printing Flexibility in Supply Chains

Summer research · USC

I worked on simulation-based models for studying flexibility in supply chains, especially settings where 3D printing can replace or supplement traditional manufacturing. The project involved stochastic simulations of assembly systems, inventory dynamics, cycle times, and throughput.

Stochastic modelingSimulationSupply chains

Outpatient Clinic Stochastic Programming Project

Course project · Stochastic programming

I modeled an outpatient clinic scheduling problem with uncertainty in patient no-shows, walk-in arrivals, and service times. The project used a two-stage stochastic programming framework to study scheduling, wait times, idle time, overtime, and rejected walk-ins.
