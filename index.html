Overview

I study operations research and optimization under uncertainty. My current interests include stochastic optimization, bilevel optimization, and derivative-free optimization.

I am especially interested in optimization methods that are mathematically grounded but still useful for real decision-making problems in healthcare operations, supply chains, and machine learning.

Research and projects

Stochastic methods for bilevel optimization

Undergraduate research · ongoing

I work on bilevel optimization methods, with a focus on derivative-free and stochastic approaches. My recent work has involved implementing and comparing methods for neural architecture search, including DARTS, StocBiO, and derivative-free bilevel search methods.

3D printing flexibility in supply chains

USC summer research · 2026

I built and analyzed stochastic simulations of assembly systems where 3D printing can replace or supplement traditional manufacturing. The work focused on inventory dynamics, cycle times, throughput, and the operational value of flexibility.

Outpatient clinic stochastic programming

Course project

I modeled outpatient clinic scheduling under uncertainty in no-shows, walk-in arrivals, and service times. The project used a two-stage stochastic programming framework to study wait time, idle time, overtime, and rejected walk-ins.
