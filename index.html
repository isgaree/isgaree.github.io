Hi, I’m Isaac Garee.

I am an undergraduate student in Industrial & Systems Engineering at the University of Cincinnati, with minors in Mathematics and Statistics.

My research interests center on optimization under uncertainty, including bilevel, stochastic, and derivative-free optimization methods. I am interested in both the theory behind optimization algorithms and how these methods can be applied to practical problems in healthcare, supply chains, and machine learning.

I have worked on research projects involving bilevel optimization for neural architecture search and stochastic models for flexible supply chains. More broadly, I am interested in using operations research to improve real-world decision-making in complex systems.

Research interests

Optimization under uncertainty

Modeling and algorithmic approaches for decision-making when systems are stochastic, partially observed, or data-limited.

Bilevel and derivative-free optimization

Methods for hierarchical decision problems, especially when the upper-level variables are discrete, mixed-integer, or expensive to evaluate.

Healthcare operations

Optimization models for scheduling, resource allocation, uncertainty, and patient-centered operational decisions.

Supply chains and machine learning

Applications of stochastic simulation, flexibility, and optimization to manufacturing systems and ML model selection.
